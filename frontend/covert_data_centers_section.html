Covert Data Centers

The typical datacenter is visible from satellites because of its cooling and electrical equipment. However, states could design datacenters that are much more stealthy. For example, the PRC might build datacenters in ordinary warehouse buildings (of which there are thousands in China), power them with shipments of natural gas, and diffuse thermal output underneath bodies of water. If the PRC built covert datacenters like these, how much energy capacity could they provide?

Breaking down covert data center capacity

The capacity of covert data centers is mainly determined by the number of workers constructing them at any given time, how early the PRC starts building covert data centers, and how much total energy the PRC consumes. We assume that the energy consumption of the datacenters doesn't affect their stealth unless they consume a meaningful fraction (--) of all PRC energy consumption, at which point covert datacenters are likely to be detected.
