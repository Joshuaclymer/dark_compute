Chip Stock Detection Parameters

US intelligence could detect a covert compute project by noticing that AI chips are missing from China's reported compute stock. Whether missing chips raise suspicion depends on two factors:

1. How many chips are diverted
If China diverts half its AI chips to a secret project, that's hard to miss. But if only 5% go missing, it might slip under the radar.

2. How precise US estimates are
If the US thinks China has "somewhere between 800K and 1.2M H100-equivalents," a few percent missing won't stand out. But if US intelligence has precise counts, even small discrepancies become obvious.

What does history tell us?
Looking at intelligence estimates of weapons counts from 1960–2025, the median error is around 15%.


Why AI chips might be easier to track
Unlike most weapons, AI chips are produced by a highly sophisticated supply chain that might be particularly easy to monitor. So we estimate US intelligence error at around 7%—better than the historical average for weapons.
