Detection Time

How long can a secret project stay hidden? It depends heavily on how many people are involved. More workers means more chances for leaks, defections, or surveillance to catch something.

We estimated the relationship between the number of workers and detection likelihood using data from 12 covert nuclear weapons programs over the past 50 years, tracking how many "high-context" workers each program employed and how long it took for outsiders to discover it.


The math
We assume detection time follows a Gamma distribution, and the mean time to detection scales inversely with the log of workforce size:

μ(N) = A / log₁₀(N)B

The variance is proportional to the mean (variance = σ² × μ), which gives the distribution its heavy tail.
