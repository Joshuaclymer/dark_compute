The secrecy tax
Building in secret takes longer. When we compared declared versus undeclared nuclear enrichment facilities, the secret ones took about 1.5x as long to build (5.5 years vs 3.7 years on average). Applying this "secrecy tax" to chip fabs suggests covert construction would take 1.5–4.5 years:



How labor affects construction speed
McKinsey estimates that fab construction requires about 100 workers per billion dollars of construction cost. If a project is understaffed, construction slows proportionally. But throwing extra workers at it doesn't speed things up—there are only so many tasks that can happen in parallel.
