PRC SME Indigenization

Why this matters for secrecy
The semiconductor equipment market has very few buyers and sellers. If China's declared chip industry suddenly had equipment missing, it would be an obvious red flag. So a covert fab would need to use mostly domestic equipment to avoid detection through procurement records.

These parameters represent the probability that China achieves >90% domestic sourcing for semiconductor manufacturing equipment at each process node by the specified year.



Where China stands today (2025)
China can manufacture 130nm chips using about 80% domestic tools. But advanced nodes still require imported equipment—especially photolithography scanners, the most critical and hardest-to-replace machines.

What might change by 2031
• 28nm: About 25% chance of reaching >90% localization. SMEE and Huawei are actively developing DUV immersion scanners.
• 14nm: About 10% chance. This node requires multi-patterning using 28nm scanners—technically possible but more challenging.
• 7nm: About 6% chance. This would require either EUV or extremely aggressive multi-patterning.

What's driving progress
• US export controls have made domestic production urgent
• The government has committed $47.5B specifically to semiconductor equipment
• Aggressive talent recruitment, with salaries reaching 3x what ASML pays
