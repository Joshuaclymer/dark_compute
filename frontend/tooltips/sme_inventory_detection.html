SME Inventory Detection

US intelligence could detect a covert fab by noticing that semiconductor manufacturing equipment is missing from China's declared chip industry. Whether missing equipment raises suspicion depends on two factors:

1. How much equipment is diverted
If 80 out of 100 scanners disappear, that's obvious. But if only a handful go missing, it might not be noticed.

2. How precise US estimates are
If the US thinks China has "somewhere between 50 and 150 DUV scanners," a dozen missing won't stand out. But if US intelligence has precise counts, even small discrepancies become obvious.

What does history tell us?
Looking at intelligence estimates of weapons counts from 1960–2025, the median error is around 15%.


Why equipment might be easier to track
China's semiconductor industry is quasi-private, with some investor visibility. And the equipment supply chain is sprawling and hard to lock down. We estimate US intelligence error at around 7%—about 2x better than for weapons programs.
