SME Inventory Detection

Method:
US intelligence could detect a covert fab by noticing missing equipment from China's domestic semiconductor industry.

Key Factors:
1. Amount diverted: Diverting 80 of 100 scanners would be obvious; a handful might not be noticed.
2. USG error bars: If estimates show 50–150 DUV scanners, a dozen missing wouldn't raise suspicion. Precise counts would reveal discrepancies.

Historical Calibration:
Intelligence estimates of weapons counts (1960–2025) show median errors of ~15%.


Adjustment:
China's semiconductor industry may be quasi-private with investor visibility, and the expansive SME industry is difficult to lock down. Estimated US intelligence error margin: ~7% (2x smaller than weapons programs).
